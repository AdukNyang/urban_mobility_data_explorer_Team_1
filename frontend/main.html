🏙 Midtown Corridor
The engine of NYC taxi demand
Midtown and Upper East Side alone account for over 35% of all January pickups — offices, hotels, and Penn Station converge here.
✈ Airport Routes
JFK & LaGuardia in the top 10
Airport trips fetch flat fares and larger tips — yet they represent only 6% of volume. The economics are outsized relative to their count.
🌆 Outer Boroughs
The sharp dropoff past the bridge
Yellow cab demand collapses in Brooklyn, Queens, and the Bronx. This January data predates the green cab expansion that would change this story.
03 — Temporal Patterns
Trips by Hour Rush hours highlighted
The city never truly sleeps — but demand troughs at 4 AM before surging again with the morning commute. Evening rush peaks harder than morning.
Trips by Day of Week Mon → Sun
Friday is the peak day. Sunday is the quietest. The weekly rhythm is remarkably consistent — until a storm or holiday breaks it.
04 — Fare & Payment Analysis
Fare Distribution Most common bucket highlighted
Most trips cost $10–$15 — a short Manhattan hop. The long tail toward $50+ is almost entirely airport runs.
Payment Type Tip % recorded for card only
Card payments dominate at ~70%. Cash tip data is unrecorded by TLC — the true tip rate is higher than the data suggests.

06 — Trip Economics
Cost per Mile vs. Trip Distance The base fare economics story
The $2.50 base fare makes short rides brutally expensive per mile — a 1-mile hop costs ~$8/mile once surcharges land. A 15-mile JFK flat-fare run drops below $4/mile. Distance is the great equalizer.
